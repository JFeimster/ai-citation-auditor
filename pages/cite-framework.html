Framework

C.I.T.E. is the filter that keeps finance audits from becoming guesswork.

The framework checks whether a finance page is clear, structurally easy to extract, trusted enough to quote, and connected to the right entity ecosystem before anyone asks AI to summarize it.

Open the GPT See examples

What C.I.T.E. means

  • Clarity - can the page answer the question fast?
  • Information structure - is the page easy to scan and extract?
  • Trust signals - does the page feel grounded and careful?
  • Entity fit - do links, schema, and CTAs support the right decision?

Signal map

The framework is blunt on purpose.

Clarity

Start with the answer, name the audience, and remove vague filler.

Information structure

Use question-style H2s, tables, lists, and short paragraphs that machines can parse.

Trust signals

Show boundaries, context, and careful wording instead of fake certainty.

Entity fit

Link to related pages that explain the topic, the risk, and the next decision.

Scorecard

Use the scorecard to decide whether the page is ready.

A good finance page usually has a direct answer, useful structure, enough context, and a CTA that matches the reader’s likely next move.

Area Healthy signal Weak signal

When to use it

Run the framework on pages that carry conversion or compliance risk.

Funding explainers

Check approval, speed, and eligibility language before it starts promising things.

Credit content

Make sure the page is careful about outcomes and user expectations.

Fintech landing pages

Ensure the page can be extracted without losing the actual offer.

Content refreshes

Use the framework when old pages need a second pass for clarity and structure.

FAQ

How strict is C.I.T.E.?

Does C.I.T.E. replace a content brief?

No. It tightens the audit and revision lens. You still need a content brief, a business goal, and a compliance review where appropriate.

Is one weak area enough to fail the page?

Not always. The point is to find the most material gaps first so you can decide whether the page is publishable or needs another pass.

Why is entity fit part of the framework?

Because internal links, schema, and CTAs shape what the page becomes in the broader site system.
