The framework

Six pillars that decide whether AI can use your page.

The four C.I.T.E. letters expand into six practical pillars the auditor pressure-tests on every page — from the first answer block to the internal-link web that holds your cluster together.

C

Answer clarity

Clarify the answer

Answer engines reward pages that state the answer fast and in plain English. If your reader has to scroll past 500 words of “AI is changing everything” to find the point, the machine gives up first.

What it checks

  • A 40–60 word direct answer near the top
  • Primary keyword in the first 100 words, naturally
  • Plain-English definition of the topic
  • A named audience and a clear outcome
  • No throat-clearing intro or fake certainty

Answer block formula

  • [Keyword] is [plain-English definition]
  • It helps [finance audience] do [outcome]
  • …by using [mechanism]
  • It matters because [trust / visibility / revenue angle]
  • It does not [unsafe overclaim]

The question AI asks: Can I quote one clean sentence from this page and be right?

I

Evidence & context

Include evidence

Finance is a trust category. Answer engines weigh signals that the advice is current, sourced, and honest about its limits. A page with no trust trail is a page AI hesitates to repeat.

What it checks

  • Author or reviewer trust signals
  • An updated date and clear methodology
  • Realistic examples, not invented case studies
  • Source notes for any stat or claim
  • Finance-specific context, not generic advice

Trust box template

  • States what the content can help with
  • States what it cannot do
  • Flags funding, credit, tax, or legal topics
  • Notes that outcomes vary by profile and criteria
  • Discloses broker, affiliate, or referral relationships

The question AI asks: Is this safe to repeat to someone making a money decision?

E

Extraction structure

Engineer for extraction

Even a great answer gets ignored if it is buried in a wall of text. Extraction structure is how you package the page so machines — and skimming humans — can lift the useful parts cleanly.

What it checks

  • Question-style H2s that mirror real prompts
  • Short paragraphs and scannable lists
  • Comparison, summary, and workflow tables
  • An FAQ block built for AI-style questions
  • Step-by-step sections where a process exists

H2 question map

  • “Introduction” → “What is [topic]?”
  • “Benefits” → “Why does [topic] matter for [audience]?”
  • “Strategy” → “How can [audience] use [topic]?”
  • “Risks” → “What should [audience] avoid?”
  • “Next steps” → “What to do before [decision]?”

The question AI asks: Can I lift a heading, table, or FAQ answer and use it as-is?

T

Financial-risk wording

Tie it to a decision, safely

This is where finance content lives or dies. Overconfident funding, credit, and approval language isn’t just risky for compliance — it makes answer engines wary of citing you at all. Tie the content to a real financial decision without promising the outcome.

What it checks

  • No guaranteed approval, funding, or rankings
  • No “AI predicts approval” style claims
  • “Best option” reframed as fit- or profile-based
  • Education, referral, brokerage, and lending kept distinct
  • Examples that don’t imply universal outcomes

Risky → safer rewrites

  • “Guaranteed approval” → “Help prepare for a funding conversation”
  • “Get funded today” → “Explore options based on current criteria”
  • “Instant qualification” → “Initial readiness review”
  • “This will improve your credit” → “May support better credit habits”
  • “AI predicts loan approval” → “AI can organize signals lenders may review”

The question AI asks: Will repeating this claim mislead someone or create risk?

E

Schema fit

Engineer for machines

Schema is structured data that tells engines what your page actually is. Used well, it reinforces clarity and structure. Used as glitter — schema that doesn’t match the visible content — it backfires.

What it checks

  • Article schema matches the visible article
  • FAQPage only when FAQs are actually on the page
  • HowTo only when visible steps exist
  • BreadcrumbList matches the real page path
  • VideoObject only when a video is embedded

Technical QA

  • Meta title and description match intent
  • Short, readable, keyword-aligned slug
  • Descriptive image alt text
  • Indexable and readable on mobile
  • No schema that overstates the content

The question AI asks: Does the structured data tell the truth about this page?

E

Internal links

Engineer the ecosystem

A lonely blog post has no authority. Internal links build a topical web that helps engines understand your expertise and guides readers toward the next useful decision — not random traffic confetti.

What it checks

  • A link to the main pillar or authority hub
  • Links to relevant spoke articles
  • Descriptive anchor text, not “click here”
  • One clear primary intent (no cannibalization)
  • A CTA that connects to a real next step

Internal link web

  • Pillar: primary authority page
  • Spokes: definition, workflow, comparison guides
  • Tool: checklist, scorecard, or calculator
  • Support: risk or FAQ page
  • Destination: the reader’s next logical decision

The question AI asks: Does this page belong to a credible, connected body of work?

FAQ

Questions about the framework.

What does C.I.T.E. stand for?

Clarify the answer, Include evidence and context, Tie it to a financial decision, and Engineer the page for extraction. Those four ideas expand into six practical pillars: answer clarity, evidence and context, extraction structure, financial-risk wording, schema fit, and internal links.

Is C.I.T.E. just SEO with a new name?

No. Traditional SEO optimizes for ranking in a list of links. C.I.T.E. optimizes for whether an answer engine can understand, trust, and safely summarize finance content — and it adds compliance-safe wording, which generic SEO frameworks ignore.

Do I have to memorize the framework to use it?

No. The AI Citation Auditor applies all six pillars for you and returns a scorecard plus specific fixes. The framework page just explains what the audit is checking and why.

Is this only for finance content?

It is strongest for finance, fintech, funding, lending, business credit, ecommerce finance, and related operator content where clarity and claim safety matter most. The structure principles apply broadly, but the wording guardrails are finance-specific.

Does C.I.T.E. guarantee I’ll get cited by AI?

No. No framework or tool can guarantee AI citations, rankings, or traffic. C.I.T.E. improves citation readiness — how understandable, extractable, and trustworthy your content is — which is the part you can actually control.
