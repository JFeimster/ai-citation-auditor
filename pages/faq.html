Frequently asked questions

What people usually want to know before they send a page for review.

What is AI citation readiness?

AI citation readiness is how prepared a finance page is for AI answer engines to understand, trust, summarize, and potentially cite it. It focuses on clarity, structure, trust signals, risky-claim control, schema fit, and internal links.

Does it guarantee citations?

No. It can improve citation readiness and clarity, but no tool can guarantee AI citations, rankings, traffic, approvals, or financial outcomes.

What is an AI search audit?

An AI search audit reviews how a page may read to answer engines, with attention to answer blocks, extraction structure, trust signals, schema, and safer wording.

What does the GPT do?

The GPT scores finance pages, flags risky claims, recommends better H2s, suggests tables and FAQs, maps internal links, and returns a publish checklist.

What does a manual audit include?

A manual audit typically covers clarity, structure, claim risk, schema fit, and internal link opportunities, plus a written set of revision notes.

Does it replace legal/compliance review?

No. It can flag wording and structure issues, but it does not replace legal, tax, underwriting, lending, credit, or compliance review.

What schema should I use?

Use schema that matches visible content. FAQPage only belongs on pages with visible FAQs, and HowTo should only be used when the page truly has sequential steps.

How do internal links help?

Internal links connect the page to a relevant hub and supporting pages, which can help readers and search systems understand the topic cluster.

What do manual audits not guarantee?

They do not guarantee AI citations, rankings, traffic, approvals, funding, credit improvement, or financial outcomes.

What should I do next?

Start with the GPT for a quick pass, then request a manual audit when the page needs a deeper review or a client-facing revision brief.
