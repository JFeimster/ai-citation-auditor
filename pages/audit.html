What the audit checks

The C.I.T.E. method, applied to your page.

A finance-specific citation-readiness review — not a generic SEO grade. Here’s what it pressure-tests before you publish.

01

Answer clarity

Checks for a direct 40–60 word answer block, a plain-English definition, and a named audience near the top.

02

Extraction structure

Reviews question-style H2s, short paragraphs, tables, FAQs, and step-by-step workflows AI can parse.

03

Finance-safe wording

Flags risky funding, credit, approval, and outcome claims and suggests safer educational rewrites.

04

Trust signals

Looks for updated dates, disclaimers, methodology, realistic examples, and source-backed claims.

05

Internal links & schema

Maps pillar/spoke link opportunities and recommends Article, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, and HowTo schema.

06

CTA alignment

Confirms the call-to-action matches the reader’s next logical step — and ends with a publish checklist.

FAQ

Sharp answers before you run the audit.

Does this guarantee AI citations or rankings?

No. It improves citation readiness, answer clarity, structure, and trust signals. It does not guarantee rankings, citations, traffic, approvals, credit outcomes, or financial results.

What should I paste in?

Paste a draft article, landing page, outline, page copy, or extracted URL text. You can also ask it to review a specific section, CTA, FAQ block, or internal-link plan.

Is this only for finance content?

It is strongest for finance, fintech, funding, lending, business credit, ecommerce finance, and related operator content where clarity and claim safety matter most.

Can this replace legal or compliance review?

No. It can flag wording that may be risky or overconfident, but it is not legal, compliance, tax, lending, credit, or financial advice.

What does the output look like?

A scorecard, missing answer blocks, improved H2s, table suggestions, risky claim rewrites, FAQ rewrites, schema recommendations, internal link additions, a CTA review, and a publish checklist.
