What it does

It checks the page the way AI answer engines and real readers will.

Score the page

Rate the page on direct answers, evidence, trust, structure, links, CTA fit, and risk.

Surface weak blocks

Find missing definitions, thin intros, awkward sections, and vague summaries before they ship.

Rewrite risky claims

Swap certainty, approval, and outcome claims for wording that is clearer and safer.

Map the next link

Point readers toward the framework, examples, or risk-claim page when more context is needed.

Suggest structure

Recommend question-style H2s, tables, and FAQs that help extraction without stuffing keywords.

End with a checklist

Ship with a final pass that calls out what to fix before the page goes live.

How it works

The workflow is simple enough to use, strict enough to be useful.

Paste the page, ask for a score, and let the GPT return a practical audit. Then use the scorecard, fixes, and checklist to clean up the page before it reaches readers or answer engines.

1. Paste the page Drop in draft copy, extracted text, or a section you want reviewed.
2. Read the score See where the page is strong, weak, or risky in plain English.
3. Fix the structure Apply the H2, table, FAQ, and internal-link suggestions.
4. Ship with less noise Use the final checklist to reduce avoidable claim and extraction issues.
