Checklist

Use this before you publish anything that touches finance decisions.

This page distills the audit into a publish checklist for funding, credit, fintech, and business-finance content. The goal is simple: make the page clearer, safer, and easier to extract.

Publish checklist

The page should answer the question, not just occupy the URL.

1. Answer block

The page gives a direct answer in the first screen and names the audience.

2. Trust note

Anything touching money, credit, or funding includes a clear boundary statement.

3. Structure

H2s, lists, and tables follow the reader’s decision path, not the writer’s keyword dump.

4. Claim safety

Guarantees, approvals, and outcome promises are rewritten before publish.

5. Internal links

The page points to the framework, examples, or a relevant supporting page.

6. CTA fit

The CTA matches the reader’s next logical step instead of forcing a leap.

Quick checks

These are the questions the audit keeps asking.

If the answer is no to any of these, the page probably needs another pass before it ships.

Can I quote the intro?If not, the answer block is too soft.
Can a machine extract the structure?If not, the H2s and tables need work.
Does the page overpromise?If yes, rewrite the claims before publish.
Does the CTA make sense?If not, it is likely the wrong next step.

High-value additions

  • A direct definition near the top.
  • A useful table or comparison block.
  • Question-style H2s and real FAQs.
  • Internal links to related pages.
  • Clear wording around limits and outcomes.

Red flags

  • Guaranteed approval language.
  • Thin copy that never gets to the point.
  • Claims that sound better than the business reality.
  • No supporting links or page context.
  • FAQs that answer questions nobody asked.

FAQ

How strict should the checklist be?

Do all finance pages need every checklist item?

No. Use judgment. The more the page influences money decisions, the more you should expect from the structure and wording.

Is a FAQ section always required?

No. Only use FAQ blocks when they answer real questions and add real value to the page.

What’s the minimum publish bar?

Direct answer, careful wording, clear structure, at least one useful support block, and a CTA that fits the page intent.

Final CTA
