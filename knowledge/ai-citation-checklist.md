# Full Checklist Outline

Use this as the actual downloadable checklist. The goal is not to make content longer. The goal is to make it clearer, safer, more useful, and worth quoting.

## 1. Answer Block Formula

- [ ] The primary keyword appears naturally in the first 100 words.
- [ ] The page includes a 40–60 word direct answer near the top.
- [ ] The topic is defined in plain English.
- [ ] The answer names the specific audience.
- [ ] The answer explains the outcome the reader wants.
- [ ] The answer includes the mechanism or method, not just a vague benefit.
- [ ] The answer avoids fake certainty, hype, or guaranteed outcomes.
- [ ] The first screen tells the reader what the page is, who it is for, and why it matters.
- [ ] The intro does not waste 500 words explaining that “AI is changing everything.” We know. The toaster knows.

## 2. Finance Trust Box

- [ ] The page includes a clear Trust Note near the top or before the first CTA.
- [ ] The Trust Note explains what the content can help with.
- [ ] The Trust Note explains what the content cannot do.
- [ ] If the topic touches funding, lending, credit, tax, legal, accounting, or underwriting, the disclaimer says so.
- [ ] The content does not imply guaranteed approvals, offers, income, savings, rankings, or financial outcomes.
- [ ] AI is framed as support for organization, education, analysis, or preparation — not as a replacement for human judgment or lender underwriting.
- [ ] Any broker, affiliate, referral, or education relationship is disclosed where relevant.

## 3. H2 Question Map

- [ ] Every major H2 answers a real user question.
- [ ] Weak headings like “Overview,” “Benefits,” and “Conclusion” are replaced with specific question-style headings.
- [ ] The page includes at least one “What is...” section if the topic needs definition.
- [ ] The page includes at least one “How does...” or “How to...” section if the topic involves a process.
- [ ] The page includes at least one “What should you avoid?” section if the topic has risk.
- [ ] The section order follows the reader’s decision path, not the writer’s keyword dump.
- [ ] FAQ questions match actual search behavior or AI prompts.

## 4. AI-Friendly Table Checklist

- [ ] The page includes at least one useful table.
- [ ] The table clarifies a decision, comparison, checklist, workflow, or risk.
- [ ] The table headings are descriptive without being bloated.
- [ ] The table avoids fake precision when criteria vary by lender, platform, product, or market condition.
- [ ] The table includes finance-specific examples.
- [ ] The table can be understood without reading the entire article first.
- [ ] The table supports the user’s next decision.

## 5. Internal Link Checklist

- [ ] The page links to the main pillar or authority hub.
- [ ] The page links to relevant spoke articles.
- [ ] Internal links use descriptive anchor text.
- [ ] Links point readers toward the next useful decision, not random blog traffic confetti.
- [ ] The page avoids keyword cannibalization by supporting one clear primary search intent.
- [ ] The CTA connects to a relevant checklist, scorecard, funding option, broker resource, automation workflow, or lead magnet.
- [ ] At least 3–6 related Moonshine / finance ops pages are identified for internal linking where available.

## 6. Compliance-Safe Wording Checklist

- [ ] “Guaranteed approval” language is removed.
- [ ] “Instant qualification” language is softened.
- [ ] “Best funding option” language is reframed as fit-based or profile-based.
- [ ] “AI predicts approval” language is reframed as AI organizing or reviewing signals.
- [ ] Tax, legal, accounting, credit, or underwriting claims are reviewed carefully.
- [ ] The article avoids invented statistics, fake case studies, fake expert quotes, and unsupported claims.
- [ ] The page distinguishes education, referral, brokerage, underwriting, and lending where relevant.
- [ ] Any examples are realistic and do not imply universal outcomes.

## 7. Article Refresh Checklist

- [ ] The article includes an updated date.
- [ ] Product details, platform features, funding criteria, lender references, and examples are current.
- [ ] Old AI platform behavior is not described as if it is still guaranteed.
- [ ] Internal links still work and point to the best current pages.
- [ ] The CTA is still aligned with the page’s search intent.
- [ ] FAQs are refreshed based on current reader questions.
- [ ] Any screenshots, embeds, or graphics still match the current product or workflow.
- [ ] High-value finance articles are reviewed at least quarterly.

## 8. YouTube Companion Checklist

- [ ] The video title mirrors the article’s search intent.
- [ ] The first 10 seconds clearly state the problem.
- [ ] The description includes the main answer in the first two lines.
- [ ] Chapters follow question-style structure.
- [ ] The video includes one clear CTA to download the checklist.
- [ ] The thumbnail hook is simple, direct, and benefit-driven.
- [ ] The script avoids risky lending, approval, credit, tax, legal, or financial outcome claims.
- [ ] The video can stand alone even if someone never reads the article.

## 9. Schema QA Checklist

- [ ] Article schema matches the visible article.
- [ ] Breadcrumb schema matches the actual page path.
- [ ] FAQ schema is only used if FAQs are visible on the page.
- [ ] HowTo schema is only used if the page includes visible step-by-step instructions.
- [ ] VideoObject schema is only added if a video is embedded or published.
- [ ] The meta title and meta description match the search intent.
- [ ] The slug is short, readable, and aligned with the primary keyword.
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text.
- [ ] The page is indexable and readable on mobile.

## 10. Do Not Publish Yet Signals

- [ ] The article has a direct answer near the top.
- [ ] The article has a Trust Note if it touches finance decisions.
- [ ] No claim implies guaranteed funding, guaranteed approval, guaranteed rankings, or guaranteed citations.
- [ ] The article includes at least one useful table or structured comparison.
- [ ] The article has a clear internal link path.
- [ ] The CTA matches the reader’s next logical step.
- [ ] The article includes specific finance examples, not generic AI advice.
- [ ] The FAQ section answers real questions.
- [ ] The page has one clear primary intent.
- [ ] The content is worth quoting. If not, do not publish yet.

```text
Trust Note: This content is educational and designed to help business owners understand [topic]. It is not legal, tax, accounting, lending, underwriting, or financial advice. Outcomes vary based on business profile, documentation, revenue, credit, cash flow, lender criteria, and market conditions.
```

## H2 Question Map Template

| Weak Heading | Better AI-Friendly Heading |
|---|---|
| Introduction | What Is [Topic]? |
| Benefits | Why Does [Topic] Matter for [Audience]? |
| Strategy | How Can [Audience] Use [Topic]? |
| Risks | What Should [Audience] Avoid? |
| Next Steps | What Should You Do Before [Decision]? |

## Risky Claim Rewrite Table

| Risky Wording | Safer Wording |
|---|---|
| “Guaranteed approval” | “Help prepare for a funding conversation” |
| “AI predicts loan approval” | “AI can organize signals lenders may review” |
| “Best funding option” | “Potentially relevant funding option based on profile” |
| “Instant qualification” | “Initial readiness review” |
| “Get funded today” | “Explore available funding options based on current criteria” |
| “This will improve your credit” | “This may support better credit management habits” |
| “AI replaces your broker” | “AI can support broker research, organization, and follow-up” |

## Internal Link Web Template

```text
Main pillar: [Primary authority page]
Supporting spoke 1: [Definition / beginner guide]
Supporting spoke 2: [Workflow guide]
Supporting spoke 3: [Comparison page]
Supporting spoke 4: [Tool / calculator / checklist]
Supporting spoke 5: [Risk or FAQ page]
CTA destination: [Checklist / scorecard / application / consultation / tool]
```
